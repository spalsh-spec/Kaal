# LeverageOS — Business Plan

## Executive Summary

LeverageOS is the world's first AI-native laptop experience for non-developers. We sell premium hardware (MacBook, Windows) pre-loaded with a curated, authenticated AI tool stack configured for specific professional verticals, then maintain and update that stack monthly through a subscription-like service layer.

**Problem:** 97% of the workforce cannot access the productivity gains of AI because the setup, curation, and maintenance of AI tools requires technical knowledge they don't have.

**Solution:** A complete, ready-to-use AI system delivered as hardware — configured for their profession, zero setup required, updated monthly.

**Revenue model:** One-time hardware sale ($1,799–$2,799) + Stack Update subscription ($49/month after included period) + consulting services ($299–$2,999 per engagement).

## Business Model

### Revenue Streams

**1. Hardware + Config (Primary)**
- Standard ($1,799): MacBook Air M3 + 3-month Stack Updates + onboarding
- Pro Bundle ($2,799): MacBook Pro M3 Pro + 6-month Stack Updates + consulting sessions
- Enterprise: Custom pricing (5+ units), volume discount, SSO/MDM

**2. Stack Update Subscription (Recurring)**
- $49/month after included period
- Renews monthly or annually ($449/yr, 23% discount)
- Net retention target: 85%+ at 12 months

**3. Consulting Services**
- Quick Win Session: $299 (60 min)
- Monthly Accelerator: $599/month (3 sessions)
- Full Transformation: $2,999 (6-month engagement)
- Target: 30% of customers purchase at least one consulting session

### Unit Economics

| Metric | Standard | Pro Bundle |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $1,799 | $2,799 |
| Hardware COGS (MacBook) | $1,099 | $1,499 |
| Config labor (2h) | $80 | $80 |
| Packaging + shipping | $45 | $45 |
| Customer acquisition cost | $120 | $120 |
| **Gross margin** | **~25%** | **~37%** |

**Consulting gross margin:** ~72% (specialist labor + platform overhead)

**Blended gross margin target (Y2+):** 42%

## Go-To-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Waitlist → Launch (Months 1–3)

- Build waitlist to 5,000 via:
  - Founder-led content on LinkedIn and X (proof of concept)
  - Landing page SEO (targeting: "AI for [profession]")
  - PR outreach to TechCrunch, The Verge, Fast Company
- Product Hunt launch on Day 1
- Direct email to waitlist: early access at 15% discount
- Goal: 200 paid units in first 60 days

### Phase 2: Organic + Influencer (Months 4–12)

- Commission profession-specific YouTube creators (1 per vertical)
- "Day in the life" video series featuring real customer workflows
- Referral program: $200 per referred purchase (active from Day 30)
- LinkedIn thought leadership: weekly posts showcasing real workflow transformations
- SEO content: 50 articles targeting profession + AI keywords

### Phase 3: Paid Acquisition (Months 7–18)

- Meta/Instagram: profession-specific creatives (doctor, lawyer, creator)
- YouTube pre-roll: testimonial-style, 60-second, targeting professionals by job title
- Retargeting: website visitors → time-sensitive offer
- Target CAC: <$120 blended

### Phase 4: Enterprise Channel (Year 2)

- Hire 2 enterprise sales reps
- Target: law firms (3–15 devices), medical groups, marketing agencies, consulting firms
- Enterprise pilot program: 3 devices free, 90-day pilot, then close
- Channel partner program: MSPs and IT consultants receive 8% commission

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Model | Why LeverageOS wins |
|---|---|---|
| Standard laptop + DIY AI | Consumer | 200h setup time; no curation; no updates |
| AI subscription apps (Jasper, Copy.ai) | SaaS | No hardware; single-use; no integrated stack |
| Corporate AI deployment (IT team) | Enterprise IT | Too expensive; too slow; not profession-specific |
| AI courses (Maven, Reforge) | Education | Knowledge only, not a system; doesn't maintain currency |

**Our defensible moat:**
1. Profession-specific configuration depth — 6 months of real customer feedback baked in
2. Monthly curation team — the tool landscape changes weekly; staying current is a full-time job
3. Hardware-as-trust — customers give us permission levels (account credentials) that pure software providers can't access
4. Community intelligence — 2,400+ users discovering new workflows that feed back into the product

| CAC rises above margin | Medium | Focus on referral and organic before paid scale |
| Customer expectations mismatch | Low | Clear 30-day return policy; strong onboarding |
